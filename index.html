BIO

Hello, I'm Chris. I graduated from California State University Monterey Bay with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science, with a  concentration in Software Engineering. I have always been interested in technology, which stemmed from playing video games; however, I became professionally interested in computers in high school after my computer got a virus. I did not have money to fix it, so I asked one of my friends in the tech club to take a look at it, and he managed to fix it. I was amazed at how easy it was for him to fix it because I always imagined it took years of school.
​
I wanted to learn to fix my computer myself, so I started researching computers on my own time and I took all the computer classes my high school had to offer, including a couple programming classes. I also started freelance repairing and selling computers. Despite all this, I majored in business, then political science at Cuesta Community College because I wanted to get a degree and a job as soon as possible.
Soon, I realized that those majors were interesting, but they didn’t offer much practical skills. So after 3 years, or about 6 semesters at Cuesta College, I finally switched my major to Computer Science and basically restarted school; but, even though I have had to spend more time in school, I think it was one of the best decisions I’ve made because everything I have learned in my Computer Science classes has been enjoyable, and practical.

PROGRAM DESCRIPTION

The Computer Science program at CSU Monterey Bay is a Bachelor’s degree program composed of upper-division courses. Students in the program have already completed the 2-years of lower-division computer science courses, thereby, the program takes 2-years to finish. It consists of 6 trimester sessions with each session containing 2 classes.
The program has an emphasis on Software Engineering. With courses such as CST336: Internet Programming, CST438: Software Engineering, CST325: Graphics Programming, and CST383: Introduction to Data Science, students will get the necessary skills to succeed in the largest growing industry of the modern world. However, with classes like CST334: Operating Systems, CST311: Introduction to Computer Networking and CST363: Database Management, students will gain a broad enough understanding of computer science to be able to branch into their desired field.
Along the way to completing the CSUMB Computer Science Bachelor’s degree program, I hope to obtain various IT certificates such as the CompTIA A+, Networking+, and Security+. This will allow me to get a job or internship, and build a career as a cybersecurity specialist. That is my long-term goal. The goal directly before, or after, graduating is to get a job in the IT industry. After achieving the program outcomes, getting a job in the IT industry should be easy.
